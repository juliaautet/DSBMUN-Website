Historical Committee

Topic 1: Redacting and passing the Universal Declaration of Human Rights.
Topic 2: Preventing the escalation of tensions between the Western democratic and Eastern Bloc states of Central and Eastern Europe.

Leo Martin

Chair

Leo Martín Castillo is our Debate Coordinator and chair of the Historical Committee. Born in Barcelona (a city which he loves), he is currently in 11th grade, has been doing MUN since 8th grade, and has attended seven conferences, including BCNMUN, HAGAMUN (The Hague, Netherlands), and WAWMUN (Warsaw, Poland). MUN has brought him incredible experiences traveling and meeting people all around Europe. Furthermore, he is interested in politics and history, and therefore, he has planned this Historical Committee, which will be, besides legendary, the perfect combination of both.

Pablo Suckow

Chair

Pablo Suckow is 17 years old, and even though this is his first time chairing, he has over four years of MUN experience. He was born in Berlin but has lived in Mallorca and Barcelona. He joined Model United Nations because one of his main interests has always been how the world is ruled nationally and internationally. He loves to meet great and fascinating new people. Pablo’s favorite: Undefendable points of view. He wants you to remember one quote: "The world is gigantic and has everything you can imagine. Even homosexual Muslims."

Luca Lövenich

Chair

Luca Lövenich is a chair of the Historical Committee. He is 16 years old and has two years of experience in MUN, attending many conferences, such as BCNMUN, BFISMUN, and WAWMUN. Ever since Luca first heard about this activity, he's been interested in participating, especially with new committees such as the historical one. That is due to his passion for reading and geopolitics of the past, especially the late middle and early modern ages. Luca enjoys learning and finding solutions for current and past issues, as well as practicing Football, Ping Pong, and Tennis. He is passionate about debating problems and how they should or could be fixed, even though it doesn't seem like it, as he's used to sticking to his argument and not backing down. Luca looks forward to attending and chairing the debate of this committee.
